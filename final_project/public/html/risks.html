Basic Assumptions and Black Swan Events

Before we talk risks, it's good to remember the basic assumptions we make when talking about blockchains and cryptocurrency. This is to highlight the basis of many of the claims we make about the security of blockchains and how they work in general. Specifically, we assume:
  1. cryptography works
  2. the Internet exists
If cryptography breaks or the Internet ceases to exist, then like most of the world, blockchains and cryptocurrency would lose their unique guarantees of safety. This trickles into the concept of “black swan events”, or unpredictable catastrophic events that often lead to collapse. A meteor destroying the Earth could be considered a black swan event, for example. These events would similarly eliminate the cryptographic safety guarantees of blockchain systems.
