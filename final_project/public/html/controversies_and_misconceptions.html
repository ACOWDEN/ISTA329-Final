Controversies and Misconceptions

Crypto Is For Criminals

A common point made about cryptocurrency is that it is used by criminals as a way to facilitate their illicit activity. It's good to remember that according to Chainalysis (a blockchain research group often cited by U.S. government officials) less than 1% of all crypto activity in 2023 was illicit.

Regardless, blockchains uniquely possess what is known as "credible neutrality". They are systems that follow a mechanism in which there exists no possibility of discrimination for or against certain users of the system. Blockchains could not possibly maintain their safety guarantees if there did exist a way to arbitrarily discriminate who can use the system and how they can use it.

Blockchains Waste Energy

Blockchains are often controversial due to their energy consumption which is required for certain networks to achieve consensus. However, this is only a legitimate concern for blockchains that use a Proof of Work consensus algorithm, which requires computers to expend energy in order to add valid blocks to the chain. Most public networks today use a Proof of Stake algorithm, which consumes less energy per transaction than a tweet on Twitter.

All Cryptocurrencies Are the Same

Cryptocurrencies are generally perceived to all be the same, each competing to be the "winner" in the race to be the next digital cash. Hence, it seems super redundant to have so many crypto assets in existence. This is a short-sighted view of cryptocurrencies that overlooks the purpose of blockchains and crypto assets in general, which is not to serve as a slightly better form of digital cash. While there exists some overlap in the crypto assets that exist today, there are various niches of blockchain applications in which a crypto asset may correspond with as a utility token. There are very few crypto assets today that exist to serve as digital cash, and most that do are stablecoins backed by deposited cash in the real world.

Cryptocurrency is Full of Scams/Exploits

Blockchains and cryptocurrency are often criticized for the "wild west" environment that exists with blockchain applications today. Specifically, cryptocurrency investors and app users are constantly subjected to the risk of losing their funds to an exploited smart contract, developers that abandon their project, or outright scammers that manage to steal money from their users. While there are bad actors that put users and their funds at risk, there is still a healthy majority of successful and thriving projects in the industry today. Decentralized finance applications alone secure over $40 billion in deposited value, and this number is constantly growing.

Cryptocurrency is Too Slow and Expensive

Those familiar with the fees incurred on some blockchain networks know that they can get uncomfortably high when the network is busy. Blockchain networks charge the same fee for transactions regardless of the amount being used, so while fees do not discriminate based on transaction size, this can pose a barrier for users who are only able to transact with smaller amounts. While years ago this was a serious issue (the issue of blockchain scalability), modern scaling solutions like layer 2 networks and other unique blockchain networks have emerged to significantly reduce the costs of transactions to less than a penny regardless of the size.

Cryptocurrency is a Ponzi Scheme

The debate around the value of cryptocurrencies like Bitcoin often stirs the question: is cryptocurrency a ponzi scheme? Lyn Alden addresses this characterization by highlighting the absence of ponzi scheme features in Bitcoin (and similarly a majority of other cryptocurrencies).

Firstly, blockchain networks like Bitcoin have no company which is yielding value to the asset, no company promising returns on the asset, and no "leader" growing the network or its popularity. Assets are priced entirely by the free market's value perception (admittedly, a large portion of which is speculative). Another key component of blockchains and cryptocurrency is their live, tamper-proof transparency of network activity and history, while one would normally expect opacity from a supposed ponzi scheme. A final crucial point is that most cryptocurrencies derive their underlying value from their overall utility, which creates demand, rather than deriving their value from artificial demand or depending on new investors for growth. For example, as more applications and users exist on a blockchain network, more transactions will be made on the network, creating more demand for the network's native asset (e.g., ETH), therefore driving the value of the asset up. This is done entirely without the involvement of a speculative investor.

NFTs Are Pointless

NFTs create a lot of controversy around their inflated value and seemingly non-existant value proposition. What this often overlooks is the infinite potential of NFTs beyond just digital art on the blockchain. NFTs can be used for blockchain applications like social media and providing user profiles, games and providing users ownership over their in-game items and their market value, and much more.
